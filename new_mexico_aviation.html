Charting Your Course: Training Pathways

FAA Certification: The Foundation

Becoming a certified Aviation Technician in the United States typically requires obtaining an Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) license from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA). This certification demonstrates that a technician has the knowledge and skills necessary to safely maintain and repair aircraft.

Community College Programs

New Mexico offers excellent community college programs that provide comprehensive training for aspiring aviation technicians. These programs often combine classroom instruction with significant hands-on experience.

Central New Mexico Community College (CNM)

CNM's Aviation Maintenance Technology Program offers a robust curriculum covering both airframe and powerplant systems. The program emphasizes practical skills development in well-equipped facilities.

According to a study published in the Journal of Aviation/Aerospace Education & Research, hands-on training is vital to technician success. (Lutte, B. (2018). Pilot supply at the regional airlines: Airline response to the changing environment and the impact on pilot hiring. Journal of Aviation/Aerospace Education & Research, 27(1), 1-22.)

Eastern New Mexico University-Roswell (ENMU-Roswell)

ENMU-Roswell's Aviation Maintenance Technology program provides another valuable pathway, offering in-depth instruction and practical application in aircraft maintenance.

Military Training

New Mexico has a significant military presence, with bases that often provide extensive technical training in aviation maintenance. Veterans with relevant military experience may find that their training can be a stepping stone towards FAA certification and civilian aviation technician roles.

Apprenticeships and On-the-Job Learning

While less common as a primary route to FAA certification, some individuals may gain experience and training through apprenticeships or on-the-job learning opportunities with aviation companies. These pathways often require a structured training program and may lead to A&P licensure over time.
